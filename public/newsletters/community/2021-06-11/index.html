An environmental comparison of PoS and PoW

The conversation around the negative environmental impact of Bitcoin and Proof-of-Work (PoW) has been trending recently. However, it has been a focal point for Tendermint and Cosmos since 2014 with Jae Kwon and Ethan Buchman's research into Proof of Stake (PoS) and blockchain consensus mechanisms resulting in the development of Tendermint Core. The launch of Tendermint Core made it the first and most widely used implementation of a Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT) consensus. Jae Kwon shared his thoughts on the topic back in 2018 at the EDCON conference in Toronto.

Today, the results are clear. The current estimated annual energy consumption (measured in TWh) of running the PoW Bitcoin network for a year is 121.86 TWh, while Ethereum consumes around 52.27 TWh per year. By comparison, the estimated yearly consumption of a Cosmos-based PoS blockchain is less than the energy consumed by Bitcoin and Ethereum in a single day (0.00046647 TWh).
