Cosmos Hub
Announcing Cosmos Hub 2.0

The original roadmap for the Cosmos Hub was completed with the integration of IBC and cross-chain transfers earlier this year. Since then, a new roadmap for the Cosmos Hub was needed. The newest vision for the second-generation Cosmos Hub is for the first time a community-led effort.

On a high-level, the Cosmos Hub 2.0 will focus on four categories:

  • Cross-Chain protocols bringing DeFi to Cosmos

  • Staking derivatives to increase the utility of ATOM

  • Interchain staking to secure new chains

  • NFTs, Governance, interchain accounts, and more

The proposal to change the Cosmos Hub inflation rate passed

Last week, the Cosmos governance proposal (prop#48) to re-evaluate and change the Cosmos Hub inflation rate passed. The goal behind changing the network's current inflation rate set-up is to protect against unbonding shocks, which could compromise the network's security.

Currently, the Cosmos Hub has a goal of maintaining a 66% ratio of bonded or staked ATOMs. A validator or delegator's staked ATOMs serve to maintain and secure the Cosmos Network. If it the ratio of staked ATOMs goes below 66%, the inflation rate and yield on staked ATOMs goes up. The result is to incentivize ATOM holders or validators to stake more ATOMs to achieve 66%. If the inverse occurs, the inflation rate goes down along with the yield. For more in-depth details, take a look at the discussion happening on the Cosmos Forum.

Starport: Build the next dApp in a $75B+ Cosmos ecosystem

Tendermint launched the first version of Starport in July 2020, marking a turning point for developers looking to build their own blockchains. Since then, Starport has undergone multiple iterations to improve the developer experience and make creating a production-ready blockchain as simple as possible.

If you've got a dApp idea in mind, Starport lets you focus on the business and ideation rather than spending precious time coding. You'll also be considering the operation cost. Today, Ethereum is too costly with transaction fees of $65, as reported in May this year. For example, if you're a blockchain game developer trying to sell $5 in-game items or $20 NFTs, choosing Ethereum as your home is simply not viable. Learn how to get your blockchain started with Starport!
